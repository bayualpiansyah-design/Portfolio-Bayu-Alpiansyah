About me

From molecules to interfaces.

b.

Hi, I'm Bayu — a product designer in Jakarta making healthcare feel less complicated.

My path wasn't a straight line: a Chemistry degree from Universitas Indonesia, a year selling lab instruments, two years running websites and SEO — then product design, where all of it finally clicked.

Since 2023 I've designed at the Digital Transformation Office (DTO) of Indonesia's Ministry of Health — collaborating with 4 cross-functional teams on 5 products in the SATUSEHAT ecosystem, from a digital prescription used by 8,000+ pharmacies to app features serving 1M+ users.

I care most about the unglamorous parts: research nobody sees, accessibility audits, and testing until the design disappears into someone's routine.

Off the clock, I tinker in Framer, chase smooth Lottie animations, and explain to relatives why I no longer wear a lab coat.

Principles I design by

01

Clarity over cleverness

If a pharmacist mid-queue has to think twice, the design failed once.

02

Accessible by default

Good design works for everyone — low vision, slow connections, and stressful days included.

03

Evidence beats ego

Every big decision gets tested with real users before it ships.
